The side-effects of smoking:

Nicotine withdrawal causes smoke addicts to be irritable and on edge when they do not smoke regularly. This can affect the moods of yourself and the people around you.

Smoking significantly increases the risk of the smoker getting lung cancer, being the highest mortality rate of all types of cancers. This is because smoking damages the sensitive lung tissue, including DNA damage.

The nicotine in smoke reduces the amount of oxygen being absorbed by your heart, raises blood pressure, and increase the chances of a blood clot, leading to heart attacks.

Here are some tips to start quitting smoking:

Tip #1: Set a quit date

Quitting can be tough, but setting a date will decrease the chances of you backing out. In the time leading up to your quit date, research on different ways to stop. (e.g counseling, text message program, etc.) If you fail, don't give up, and start again.

Tip #2: Choose a reason for quitting

Having a reason will significantly increase the chance of you sticking to your quit plan. Some common reasons why people choose to quit are to:

  • Save money
  • Live a healthier and longer life
  • Set a good example for their children

    • Tip #3: Learning to deal with failure

    You might give in to temptation and smoke a cigarette after a week. That is fine, but learning to not give up after failing is the hardest step of quitting.

    Learning to get back up is the best way to ensure success at the end of your journey to a smoke-free lifestyle.
